## What is "MIT PRD Level"?

**MIT-level PRD** = Product Requirements Document that demonstrates:

1. **Strategic Thinking** (not just requirements)
2. **Decision Frameworks** (not just decisions)

#### 11.4 Prompt Engineering Techniques: MIT Principles

**Four Advanced Thinking Frameworks Demonstrated:**

1. **First Principles Thinking** (Elon Musk's Approach)
   - Challenge: "Translation needs complex neural architectures"

   - Result: Simpler, skill-based architecture

2. **Inversion Thinking** (Charlie Munger's Approach)
   - Normal: "How to achieve 85% coverage?"
   - Inverted: "What would cause us to MISS 85%?"
   - Result: Identified all failure modes, addressed each

3. **Constraint-Based Creativity** (MIT Media Lab)
   - Budget <$1 ‚Üí Local embeddings (innovation)
   - 85% coverage ‚Üí Comprehensive mocking (quality)
   - Result: Novel architecture FROM constraints

4. **Systems Thinking** (MIT System Dynamics)
   - Linear assumption: "More noise ‚Üí proportionally more drift"
   - Systems reality: Non-linear, feedback loops
   - Result: Discovered 25% noise is recoverable
